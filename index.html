The Bridge Project




The conservation field can be hard to break into. Employers across the sector, from federal land management agencies to nonprofit conservation groups, environmental organizations, natural resource management companies, and the outdoor recreation industry struggle to connect with a talented, diverse pool of potential employees. In partnership with private and public sector leaders, the Bridge Project was developed to create inclusive and innovative hiring pathways that embolden and diversify conservation employment practices.



On May 11 and 12, 2021, The Bridge Project held a virtual two-day peer learning and hiring event to connect qualified potential employees and employers in the conservation field. This post-event evaluation includes a summary of findings and feedback from first year participants, employers, and candidates that will inform and enhance future learning and hiring events.

What improvements could be made to support candidates in the next round of facilitated hiring by The Bridge Project?

Have the candidates and coaches connect much sooner. My coached reached out to me a day before my interview, so we did not have time to connect/share. Also, sharing important information a little bit more in advance. A lot of communication came the day of or the day before, but it would be nice to have more time prepare.

Give a time frame of when candidates will hear back from employers once the hiring event is over
Maybe having more interview opportunities. I felt like I was not set up for success by interview for positions that wanted more experience. I would also love to see more variety in employer locations, but also realize it's hard to find willing employers!


What improvements could be made to support employers in the next round of facilitated hiring by The Bridge Project?

More advance communications around preparing job descriptions, interview questions, and mitigating bias. Spread it out over more time to account for other work that was piling up while we were in trainings.

I would like to see interview times slots spaced out more, even 15-30 minutes. Back-to-back interviews without a break is tough.

The curation of the candidates was interesting. We reviewed some where it was not clear to us why they were referred to us based on the positions available. It would be good to know whether an applicant specifically wanted to be considered for the position we posted or whether their referral was based purely on the perspective of someone at the Bridge Project
